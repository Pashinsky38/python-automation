üåü 1. General Information About Koalas

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia, known for their distinctive appearance and tree-dwelling habits. They have a stout, tailless body and large, fluffy ears. Koalas primarily feed on eucalyptus leaves, which provide them with most of their water needs.

They are mostly nocturnal and spend a significant amount of time sleeping, often up to 18-22 hours a day. Koalas are an iconic symbol of Australian wildlife and are protected by law due to habitat loss and other environmental threats.

üìè Physical Characteristics

Size: 60-85 cm (24-33 inches)
Weight: 4-15 kg (9-33 lbs)
Fur: Thick, woolly, gray with white chest

üß¨ Scientific Classification

Scientific Name: Phascolarctos cinereus
Family: Phascolarctidae
Order: Diprotodontia

üå≥ 2. Habitat and Distribution

Koalas are found exclusively in Australia, primarily along the eastern and southeastern coasts. They inhabit eucalyptus forests and woodlands, where they spend most of their lives in trees.

üó∫Ô∏è Geographic Range

  • Queensland: Largest population, diverse habitats
  • New South Wales: Coastal and inland forests
  • Victoria: Cooler climate adaptations
  • South Australia: Limited populations on Kangaroo Island

üè† Habitat Requirements

Koalas require specific eucalyptus species for food and shelter. They prefer trees that are at least 4-5 years old and need access to multiple tree species for a balanced diet. Climate, soil quality, and tree density all affect habitat suitability.

Koalas are specialized feeders with a diet consisting almost entirely of eucalyptus leaves. They have evolved unique adaptations to process these tough, low-nutrition leaves.

üò¥ 4. Behavior and Lifestyle

Koalas are known for their sedentary lifestyle, sleeping 18-22 hours per day. This low-energy lifestyle is an adaptation to their low-nutrition diet.

üïí Daily Activities

Sleep Patterns

Koalas sleep in tree forks, wedged securely between branches. Their sleep is so deep that they rarely fall, even during strong winds!

Social Behavior

Generally solitary animals, koalas communicate through scent marking and vocalizations. Males have a distinctive bellowing call during mating season.

Movement

Excellent climbers with strong claws and opposable digits. They rarely come to ground except to move between trees.

üë∂ 5. Reproduction and Life Cycle

Koalas have a unique reproductive system typical of marsupials, with a relatively long development period in the mother's pouch.

ü§± Breeding Cycle

Mating Season: September to February
Gestation: 35 days
Pouch Time: 6-7 months
Weaning: 12 months

üë∂ Development Stages

Birth: 2cm, hairless, blind
6 months: Emerges from pouch
12 months: Rides on mother's back
2-3 years: Sexual maturity

üçº Unique Feeding Behavior

Young koalas eat a special substance called "pap" - partially digested eucalyptus from their mother's digestive system. This helps them develop the necessary gut bacteria to digest eucalyptus leaves.
